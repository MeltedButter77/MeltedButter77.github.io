Displaying Information to Users

The Problem

In previous projects, I implemented many commands that displayed information to users. However, this approach often left users uncertain about their permissions and capabilities within their voice channels, as well as unsure how to use the bot effectively.

The Solution

To address this, I created a persistent message in each channelâ€™s associated text channel. This message displayed key information about the voice channel and included interactive buttons to modify the temporary voice channel settings. This approach provided users with immediate, clear feedback on what actions they could take and how to control their channels. While this worked well for basic management, I wanted to further improve the system to provide a more dynamic and intuitive way to lock or hide channels, as well as to whitelist or blacklist specific users from accessing public channels.

Results

The result was a clear, user-friendly interface that gave channel owners greater control over who can view or use their temporary voice channels. This improved user experience and reduced confusion by making permissions and channel status instantly visible and easily adjustable.

What I Learned

The method used to communicate functionality to users was a key aspect of this project. By creating a persistent message in the associated text channel, I was able to provide users with immediate feedback on what actions they could take and how to control their channels. This approach not only improved the user experience but also provided a more dynamic and intuitive way to manage multiple features with complex interactions.
